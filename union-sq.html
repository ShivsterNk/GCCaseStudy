To get to Union Square, the subway is the fastest choice. Thereâ€™s two different options, both having their advantages and drawbacks.

Exit from the LIRR Platform your train arrives on.

Head to the mezzanine, then walk to and take the 45th Street escalator bank up to the concourse.

Elevator: Take the elevator to the mezzanine. Then, take the elevator located between the 46th and 47th Street areas, up to the concourse.

Make your way WEST towards 43rd Street on the concourse, following signs for the subway.

You have two different ways to grab the subway. Pick and take whatever suits your needs:


Look for the entrance saying One Vanderbilt, Subway. These have clear glass doors.

Go through the doors, and go up the stairs, escalator or elevator (floor -2). Then walk straight to the turnstyle area. Pay and enter the subway!

From the turnstyle entrance, make a left, walk through the passageway, following signs for the 4/5/6 trains.

Look for the entrance saying Main Concourse, Dining Hall, Subway. These have blue doors, located on the left side.

Go through the doors, take the escalator up to the dining hall. Once there, walk and take the ramp towards the Oyster Bay restaurant.

At the intersection, make a left. Walk straight up towards the Lexington Avenue-42nd Street passageway. Make a right, then follow the signs towards the subway.

Get to the subway entrance, head for the turnstyles, pay and enter the subway!


Once you arrive, head towards the Downtown platforms for the andtrains.

Board a Brooklyn-bound orexpress train, take either train one stop to 14th Street Union Square.

Get off the train and exit.

Head towards One Vanderbilt (glass doors), and make your way up by the escalator or elevator. Then walk straight to the turnstyle area. Pay, and enter the subway!

Once inside, find the platform for theShuttle train. Board the train, take it to Times Square-42nd Street.

When you get off, follow the signs for the Broadway Line () trains, which is located near the shuttle platform.

Via the ramps, elevators, and/or stairs, go to the DOWNTOWN platform, get on any train, and take it to 14th Street-Union Square.

Get off the train at 14th Street and exit.

Some Tips and Tricks

*Before you board your LIRR train to Grand Central, board towards the front, which will bring you closest to the subway.

**If your one with ADA-needs, board towards the center of the train. This is where the elevators are located at Grand Central, giving you the shortest travel time.

At Grand Central, there is a set of elevators that will take you up from the mezzanine to the concourse.

It's located between the 46th Street and 47th Street escalator banks on the mezzanine, so look for signs.

Subway Tips

A Subway ride costs 2.90. Pay using a MetroCard, or contactless payment(OMNY Card, Credit/Debit, or your Phone).

If you need to buy a MetroCard or OMNY Card, machines are located around the turnstyle area.

If you take Option 2, board theShuttle train towards the front, ensuring you the quickest exit.

BE AWARE: At Times Square on theShuttle train, there is a gap between the train and platform. Be careful when exiting or entering the train.

From the One Vanderbilt entrance near theShuttle train, you can reach theortrains, by walking the passageway in 3-5 minutes.

Expected Time Taken - Minutes

To get to the 43rd Street Concourse from the LIRR tracks, it will take you roughly 5-7 minutes depending on your speed, where you get off, and how you get there.

To go by One Vanderbilt from the 43rd Street hall to the turnstyle area, takes 3-5 minutes, depending on your speed.

To go by Dining Concourse and 42nd Street-Lex Avenue passageway from the 43rd Street hall to the turnstyles, takes 6-8 minutes.

Using Option 1 via theandtrains from Grand Central to 14th Street, will take roughly 10 minutes.

Using Option 2 via theShuttle andortrains from Grand Central to 14th Street, will take roughly 15-18 minutes.

NOTE: Option 2 is ADA-Friendly, as theplatform at 14th Street does NOT have elevators.
